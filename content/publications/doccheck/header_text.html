DocCheck Interview: Exercise and Inflammation


It probably doesn't need to be explained to anyone anymore that exercise may be the single most potent medical intervention ever known for the most of us.

But what it has in common with inflammation, which mechanisms make it so valuable for the body, and for which patients it is contraindicated, you can find out here. The inflammatory response is a complex process whose regulation involves, among other things, immunocompetent cells, antibodies, and inflammatory mediators.

As a result of physical exercise, physical fittness and performance improves and the body undergoes remodeling processes, including e.g. mitochondrial biogenesis of the stressed muscles and adaptation of the heart and circulatory system. An individually adapted (endurance) exercise program can also contribute to the prevention of diseases, especially cardiovascular diseases. But what do exercise and inflammation have in common, and what role cytokines (e.g. IL-6) play here?

Some aspects are explained here.
